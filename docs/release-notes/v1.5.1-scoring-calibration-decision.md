# v1.5.1 Scoring Calibration Decision

## Decision

**No threshold changes in v1.5.1.**

## Evidence reviewed

- Evidence pack from `validate_v150_scoring.py` (5 repos, 25/25 checks pass)
- Evidence pack from `validate_v151_scoring_calibration.py` (Pharabius self-analysis, PASS)
- Calibration fixtures (14 boundary cases, 25 tests, all pass)
- Threshold documentation in `calibration_expectations.json`

### Observed behavior

The "score convergence" pattern is **not a threshold problem**. It occurs because:

1. Most files in the validation repos have Low centrality (no architecture graph) and Low frequency (few git commits touching their paths).
2. Enhanced scoring replaces the `architecture_centrality` and `change_frequency` values in the breakdown, which were both already at default Low (1) in the base template.
3. The net effect is that enhanced scoring with Low/Low factors produces a score of 4 for most findings, regardless of their original per-rule severity.

This is a **design characteristic** of the v1.5.0 opt-in scoring model: the base template has fixed values for non-enhanced factors, and enhanced scoring can only modify the two factors it controls. Findings with different severity profiles but identical centrality/frequency profiles will converge.

### Threshold assessment

| Factor | Current thresholds | Assessment |
|---|---|---|
| Architecture centrality | Low ≤ 2, Medium 3–5, High > 5 | Conservative and appropriate |
| Change frequency | Low 0–2, Medium 3–10, High > 10 | Conservative and appropriate |

No threshold boundary produces unexpected behavior. The boundaries correctly separate:
- Low fan-in (peripheral modules) from medium (connected modules) from high (hub modules)
- Low change frequency (stable files) from medium (moderate churn) from high (hotspots)

## Rationale

Current thresholds are conservative and produce no false classifications at the boundary level. The score convergence effect is architectural (how enhanced scoring interacts with the fixed base template), not threshold-driven. Changing thresholds would not fix convergence and would add score churn risk without evidence of improvement.

## Follow-up

- Continue collecting field evidence from more repositories before any v2.0 default scoring decision.
- Consider a deeper design review of how enhanced scoring factors interact with per-rule breakdowns (potential v1.6+ work).
- Score convergence should be re-evaluated when repositories have architecture graphs and significant git history for finding locations.
